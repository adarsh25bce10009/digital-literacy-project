SOCIAL MEDIA DO'S AND DON'TS FOR COLLEGE STUDENTS
===================================================


DO'S
----

1. Keep your profile professional. Use a clear, appropriate profile picture and a bio that you would be comfortable with a future employer or professor seeing.

2. Think before you post. Ask yourself if you would be okay with your parents, professors, or a recruiter seeing the post. If not, do not post it.

3. Use privacy settings. Regularly review who can see your posts, stories, and personal information on each platform.

4. Give credit when sharing others' work. If you repost someone's photo, writing, or idea, tag or mention the original creator.

5. Report harassment or abusive content. Use the platform's reporting tools if you or someone you know is being targeted. Do not ignore it.


DON'TS
------

1. Do not share your personal details publicly. Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID, or daily schedule on public accounts.

2. Do not post or share anything during an argument. Emotional posts made in anger are difficult to take back and can damage your reputation.

3. Do not spread unverified information. Always check a source before sharing news, especially anything that seems shocking or politically sensitive.

4. Do not accept follow or friend requests from people you do not know. Fake accounts are commonly used for scams, phishing, and harassment.

5. Do not use social media to complain about your college, professors, or classmates by name. Even if your account feels private, screenshots travel fast.
