STAY SAFE ONLINE: PREVENTION CHECKLIST
For College Students in India
=======================================


GENERAL ONLINE SAFETY
----------------------

1. Use a strong, unique password for every important account. Do not use your name, date of birth, or college roll number as a password. A mix of letters, numbers, and symbols works best.

2. Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA) on your email, social media, and any platform that offers it. This adds a second layer of protection even if someone gets your password.

3. Never click on links sent through SMS, WhatsApp, or email from unknown numbers, even if the message claims to be from a bank, college, or government department. Go to the official website directly instead.

4. Do not connect to public Wi-Fi at cafes, malls, or railway stations for anything sensitive like banking, email login, or entering passwords. Use your mobile data instead.

5. Regularly check which apps have access to your camera, microphone, contacts, and location on your phone. Revoke permissions for apps that do not need them.

6. Do not share OTPs with anyone, ever. No bank, UPI app, or government body will ever call you and ask for an OTP. Hang up immediately if someone does.


UPI AND FINANCIAL SAFETY
-------------------------

7. Remember that scanning a QR code or approving a collect request on any UPI app sends money out of your account. You do not need to scan anything to receive money. If someone tells you to scan a code to get a payment, it is fraud.

8. Always verify the UPI ID or phone number before sending money, even to someone you know. Fraudsters sometimes impersonate friends or family by creating similar-looking contact names.


IDENTITY AND ACCOUNT SAFETY
-----------------------------

9. Do not post photos of your college ID card, Aadhaar card, or any government document on social media, even partially. These can be used for identity theft.

10. If you receive a suspicious call, message, or email that pressures you to act immediately, stop and take a moment. Urgency is the most common tactic used in online fraud. Legitimate organisations will always give you time.


=======================================
REPORTING CHANNELS

If you or someone you know has been a victim of cybercrime:

National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: cybercrime.gov.in
Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (available 24x7, toll-free)

Report as soon as possible. The faster a complaint is filed, the higher the chance of recovering lost money.
